# Vision

## Why This Exists

Most product management resources are either:

- written by PMs for PMs
- too abstract for builders
- too fragmented to follow as a learning path

This project aims to close that gap by teaching product management in language that makes sense to engineers and designers.

## What This Should Become

A living curriculum that can be read in order, browsed by topic, and revisited over time.

The long-term experience should support:

- sequential learning
- external reading checklists
- lesson completion
- flashcards and review
- practical case studies

## What This Should Not Become

- a generic blog archive
- a dumping ground for article summaries
- a platform that is harder to write for than it is to learn from

## Core Promise

If a builder follows this resource from start to finish, they should come away with clearer product judgment, stronger language for trade-offs, and a better understanding of how product teams actually work.
