# Section 1 — The PM at the Intersection

## Learning Goal

Understand why Product Management exists and how it balances customer needs, technical feasibility, and business goals.

## Reading

### The PM at the intersection of Business, Technology and UX

Read:

https://www.svpg.com/the-intersection-of-business-technology-and-user-experience/

Focus on:

- Desirability
- Feasibility
- Viability
- Why great products require all three perspectives

---

### Build a scalable product system

https://review.firstround.com/to-build-great-products-build-this-strong-scalable-system-first/

Focus on:

- Hypothesis-driven product development
- Measuring outcomes instead of shipping features
- Building learning loops

---

### What Makes a Great Product Manager

https://informedpm.com/posts/great-product-manager

Focus on the core responsibilities of a PM:

- Analyze
- Prioritize
- Communicate
- Integrate
- Explain
- Make decisions

---

## Key Takeaways

- Product Managers sit at the intersection of Business, Technology, and UX.
- PMs rarely have direct authority—they create alignment through communication and evidence.
- The role is outcome-focused rather than delivery-focused.
