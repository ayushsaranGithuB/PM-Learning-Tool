This will teach you the difference between:

- Delivery Teams: These are software factories. Given a backlog of things to build and they go deliver it.
- Feature Teams: These teams are like mercenaries. They just build what others in the company tell them to build. They treat stakeholders in the company as if they are the customers. Head of marketing or sales, etc.
- Empowered Product Teams: Instead of being given roadmaps with features to build, you are instead given problems to solve. They focus on business and customer outcomes, not code output.

## The Product Triad

Origins of the Product Triad: Desirable, Viable, Feasible
The product triad takes its roots from the early 2000s, when a set of principles for innovation began to emerge.

IDEO popularized these principles in its DVF framework:

- **Desirable:** Any successful new product needs a market of people willing to use it.
- **Viable:** The business model needs to be sound over a long enough term to be profitable.
- **Feasible:** The company should be able to execute the idea behind the product.
  Focus on how Product, Design and Engineering share ownership of outcomes.

As its name suggests, the triad consists of three members: a designer, a product manager, and a software development lead. This is why the triad is sometimes called the three-legged-stool model — in contrast to a wobbly "two-legged stool” supported only by engineering and business.

## Key Takeaways

- Delivery Teams build what they're told.
- Feature Teams optimize for output.
- Empowered Product Teams solve customer problems.
- Great products come from strong collaboration between Product, Design and Engineering.
