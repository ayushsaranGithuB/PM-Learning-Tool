# Why this notebook

I compiled this study guide for myself.
Maybe like me you're thinking of taking one of the paid PM courses from Reforege or Product School, but these courses cost a lot of money and before you get into them you want to know if a Product role is actually something you might enjoy, before comitting serious money towards it.

# Know your enemy - Also for non PMs

If you've worked in engineering or design you know the common frustrations that go with that role. Sometimes it can feel like management and stakeholders just dont "get it". Unreasonable scope, Unrealistic timelines, Building features that dont work and designing products that no one uses.

> > to hunt a bison you need to be able to think like a bison.

Being able to understand the mentality and motivations of stakeholders can help us better communicate with them on their terms and present our objections and doubts in a way that registers with them and helps us feel heard.

## Learning Objective

The goal of this notebook is to help shift your mindset from someone who builds solutions to someone who investigates problems.

By the end of this module, you should understand what Product Managers do, how they work with design and engineering, and why they are needed.

After understanding these concepts you should also be able to differentiate high-performing product organizations from feature factories and tailor your job search towards roles where your chances of making a meaningful contribution are higher and hence more rewarding financially and mentally.

# How to use this guide

Lessons are presented in a linear format with recommended free reading material top PM resources on the web followed by a summary of key takeaways and a short quiz to test if you really understood the concepts of the module.

The notebook starts with trying to explain the PM mindset and arechetype and then builds from there to take you through discovery, customer research, prioritization, strategy, analytics and execution.
