# Project Brief

## Vision

Create a structured learning resource for engineers and designers who want to become stronger product thinkers.

This project should feel like a guided curriculum, not a loose collection of blog posts. At the same time, it should remain easy to grow one lesson at a time while the author is still learning.

## Audience

- Software engineers exploring product management
- Designers who want stronger product judgment
- Technical founders who want a better PM toolkit
- Builders switching into PM roles

## Product Philosophy

- Content first. Technology second.
- Lessons must stand on their own.
- The site should support a start-to-finish learning path.
- External reading should deepen a lesson, not replace it.
- Flashcards should reinforce decision-making, not rote memorization.

## Teaching Philosophy

Every lesson should:

- begin with a practical problem or story
- explain why the concept matters before naming frameworks
- include at least one memorable mental model
- connect back to real product decisions
- feel approachable to an engineer or designer with no formal PM background

## Scope For This Repo Phase

This initial phase is deliberately small:

- define the curriculum shape
- define the writing standards
- create content folders
- create starter lessons
- keep everything portable to Astro or another static site later

## Future Site Direction

The likely implementation path is Astro with content collections and MDX, plus light client-side interactivity for:

- progress tracking
- reading checklists
- flashcards
- lesson navigation

No backend is required for the first version.
